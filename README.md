# Wind Energy Forecasting & Carbon Intensity Reduction

## Overview

This project investigates the relationship between wind energy generation and carbon intensity in the UK energy sector using statistical analysis and machine learning techniques in R.

## Objectives

- Analyse the impact of wind generation on carbon intensity
- Forecast renewable energy trends
- Evaluate implications for net-zero targets
- Provide evidence-based insights for energy planning

## Tools Used

- R
- R Markdown
- ggplot2
- dplyr
- caret
- Random Forest
- XGBoost

## Key Findings

- Identified a strong negative relationship between wind generation and carbon intensity
- Analysed renewable energy scenarios and their effect on emissions
- Produced evidence-based insights supporting net-zero transition planning

## Business Impact

This project demonstrates how renewable energy analytics and machine learning can be used to support evidence-based decision making in the energy sector. The findings provide insights into how increasing wind energy generation can contribute to reducing carbon intensity and supporting net-zero objectives.
